STAGE 01

Raw Material Inspection & Mill Certificate Verification

All incoming structural steel plates, sections, pipes, and welding consumables undergo physical, dimensional, and chemical validation against original Mill Test Certificates (MTC) prior to shop release.

Material Verification: Heat number stamping match & ultrasonic flaw check for heavy plates.
Dimensional Check: Thickness tolerances, flatness, camber & squareness verification.
Traceability: Color coding and unique job card tagging across the workshop.
STAGE 02

Structural Fabrication & Welding Quality Control

Fabrication is carried out strictly per approved shop drawings and Welding Procedure Specifications (WPS/PQR) under AWS D1.1 and ASME Section IX codes.

Qualified Welders: 100% qualified welding personnel tested under 3G/4G/6G positions.
Joint Fit-Up Hold Points: Root gap, bevel angle, and alignment verified prior to root run.
Interpass Control: Pre-heat and interpass temperature monitoring via calibrated pyrometers.
STAGE 03

Non-Destructive Testing (NDT) & Volumetric Examination

Volumetric and surface NDT examination conducted by certified ASNT Level II inspectors to guarantee 100% joint integrity.

Ultrasonic Testing (UT): Full penetration groove weld inspection for internal flaws.
Magnetic Particle (MPI): Surface flaw detection on critical load-bearing structural joints.
Radiographic Testing (RT): Spot/100% RT examination for pressure piping spools.
STAGE 04

Surface Preparation (Sa 2.5) & Protective Coating Application

Abrasive blast cleaning followed by multi-coat airless spray painting applied according to client-approved coating systems and SSPC/NACE standards.

Surface Profile Check: Blast profile measurement (50–75 microns) using surface comparator.
Environmental Logging: Dew point, ambient temp & relative humidity logged before spray.
DFT Verification: Digital magnetic gauge verification for primer, intermediate & topcoats.
STAGE 05

Final Pre-Dispatch Quality Control & Client/TPA Sign-off

Dimensional audit, visual check, coating DFT audit, and document dossier verification in the presence of Client & Third-Party Inspection Agencies (TPA).

Trial Assembly: Fitment trial for complex technology structures prior to shop dispatch.
TPA Inspection: Inspection clearance by agencies such as BV, TUV, RITES, or DNV.
Document Dossier: As-built drawings, MTCs, NDT reports, and ITP sign-offs bundled.
STAGE 06

Logistics, Lashing & Site Dispatch

Careful packaging, identification tagging, heavy lashing, and transport coordination for safe delivery to project sites across India.

Member Marking: Hard punching and high-visibility paint marking as per erection key plan.
Coating Protection: Wooden dunnage and rubber wrapping to prevent transport damage.
Dispatch Clearance: Final Shipping Release Note (SRN) issued upon loading.
